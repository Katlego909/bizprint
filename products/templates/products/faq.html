üé® Artwork & Files

What file formats do you accept?

We accept PDF, PNG, and JPG files. PDF is preferred for the best print quality. Maximum file size is 5MB.

What resolution should my files be?

For best results, use 300 DPI (dots per inch) at the actual print size. Lower resolution may result in pixelated or blurry prints.

Should I use RGB or CMYK?

CMYK is preferred for accurate color reproduction in print. RGB colors may shift slightly when printed as they need to be converted to CMYK.

What is bleed and do I need it?

Bleed is extra image area beyond the trim edge (typically 3mm on all sides). It prevents white edges if there's slight movement during cutting. We recommend including bleed for professional results.

Can you design for me if I don't have artwork?

Yes! We offer professional design services with various packages to suit your budget and timeline. Our designers will create custom artwork based on your brief.

Do you check my files before printing?

Yes, we perform a basic quality check. If we notice issues (low resolution, incorrect size, etc.), we'll contact you. However, you're ultimately responsible for your file's content and quality.

‚ùì Quality & Issues

What if my order arrives damaged?

Take photos of the packaging and damaged items, don't discard packaging, and report within 24 hours. We'll arrange a replacement at no cost. See Returns & Refunds for details.

What if there's a printing error?

We'll reprint your order at no charge if the error is on our end. Contact us within 7 days with photos and your order number.

Why do the colors look different from my screen?

Screens use RGB color while printing uses CMYK. Some variation is normal. For color-critical projects, we recommend ordering a proof first.

Do you offer sample prints or proofs?

For large orders, we recommend ordering a small quantity first to verify quality. Digital proofs are provided for design service orders.
